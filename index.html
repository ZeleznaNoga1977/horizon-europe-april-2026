30-Day Action Plan

A strategic timeline for applicants with one month until the deadline wave

Week 1: Foundation

Now — 23 March 2026
  • Finalize consortium composition and confirm partner commitments in writing
  • Assign clear roles: coordinator, WP leaders, budget officer, ethics lead
  • Complete Part B outline with section responsibilities and word limits
  • Begin drafting the Excellence section — objectives, methodology overview
  • Register on the Funding & Tenders Portal and start Part A administrative forms

Week 2: Drafting

23 — 30 March 2026
  • Draft the full Excellence section — align methodology with evaluation criteria
  • Prepare budget estimates per partner using EC cost model templates
  • Begin Impact section — expected outcomes, dissemination and exploitation plan
  • Compile CVs and track records for key personnel
  • Check eligibility conditions (country requirements, partnership rules)

Week 3: Refinement

30 March — 6 April 2026
  • Complete Impact section with quantified KPIs and sustainability plan
  • Draft Implementation section — work packages, Gantt chart, milestones, deliverables
  • Internal review round — circulate to all partners for feedback
  • Address AI disclosure requirements (mandatory since 2024)
  • Prepare risk assessment table and ethics self-assessment

Week 4: Finalization

6 — 14/15 April 2026
  • Final editorial review — consistency, language, formatting, page limits
  • Complete ethics self-assessment and attach required ethics documents
  • Finalize Part A administrative forms and budget tables in the portal
  • Upload Part B PDF and verify all attachments in the submission system
  • Submit at least 48 hours before the deadline — allow buffer for technical issues
  • Celebrate and prepare for potential evaluation rebuttal phase

Key Tips for Applicants

Essential guidance to strengthen your Horizon Europe proposal

🤖

AI Disclosure Requirements

Since 2024, Horizon Europe requires disclosure of AI use in proposal writing. Include a clear statement in Part B about any generative AI tools used, their purpose, and how outputs were verified. See the EC Living Guidelines on Responsible Use of Generative AI.

✅

Evaluation Criteria Reminder

For RIA and IA proposals, the standard evaluation weighting is: Excellence (typically 50%), Impact (30%), Implementation (20%). Each criterion is scored 0–5. Minimum threshold is 4/5 for Excellence and 3/5 for the other two. Align your writing to these weights.

🤝

Consortium Building

Most RIA/IA calls require minimum 3 independent legal entities from 3 different EU Member States or Associated Countries. Prioritize complementary expertise over geography. Check Partner Search on the EC portal.

💰

Budget Planning

Typical RIA funding rate is 100% of eligible costs. IA funding rate is 70% (100% for non-profit entities). Use direct personnel costs + 25% flat rate for indirect costs. Budget should match the ambition described in the proposal.

📋

Two-Stage vs Single-Stage

For two-stage calls in CL5/CL6, the April deadline is for short proposals (~10 pages). Focus on concept, novelty, and consortium rationale. Full proposals are only invited for Stage 2 (typically October). Don't over-elaborate — concise impact statements win.

🔍

Partner Search Tools

Leverage Horizon Results Platform, CORDIS, and NCP networks to find experienced partners. Also check enterprise.europe.network for SME partners.
